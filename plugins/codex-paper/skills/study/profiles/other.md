# Other Paper Profile

## Use when
The paper does not fit the other profiles or combines several modes without one dominant profile.

## Required evidence
- Research problem and motivation.
- Main method, argument, or artifact.
- Strongest support offered by the paper.
- Scope and limitations.
- Any evidence gaps caused by parsing quality.

## Required reasoning checks
- What type of claim is each central statement?
- Which profile-specific checks still apply?
- Where does evidence become too thin for a confident conclusion?

## Common weakest assumptions
- The chosen framing captures the actual contribution.
- Evidence from one profile is being overextended to another profile.
- Missing sections hide key limitations.

## Minimal reproduction
Choose the smallest artifact that tests the central claim in its actual modality.

## Strong counterexample
A case that directly challenges the core claim rather than an implementation detail.

## Suitable learning artifacts
Code demo, interactive demo, argument map, taxonomy explorer, or simulation.

## Do not force
Do not squeeze the paper into an empirical or theoretical pattern without evidence.

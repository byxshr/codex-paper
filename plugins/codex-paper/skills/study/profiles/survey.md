# Survey Paper Profile

## Use when
The paper synthesizes a field, taxonomy, trend, or literature map rather than proposing a single new method.

## Required evidence
- Inclusion and exclusion criteria.
- Taxonomy dimensions.
- Coverage by period, venue, method, or topic.
- Claimed gaps and missing directions.
- Selection-bias or scope limitations.

## Required reasoning checks
- Why is this taxonomy useful?
- What gets hidden by the chosen categories?
- Which omitted works or directions would change the synthesis?

## Common weakest assumptions
- The selection process is representative.
- Taxonomy axes are orthogonal enough to explain the field.
- Important subfields were not excluded.

## Minimal reproduction
Reclassify a small sample of papers and check whether the taxonomy remains stable.

Support and falsification criteria may be coverage, inclusion/exclusion, classification-stability, or omitted-cluster checks.

## Strong counterexample
A cluster of omitted or misclassified work that changes the main synthesis.

## Suitable learning artifacts
Taxonomy explorer, argument map, or coverage table.

## Do not force
Do not invent experiments or ablations.

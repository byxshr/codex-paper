# Position Paper Profile

## Use when
The paper primarily argues for a framing, agenda, warning, or research direction.

## Required evidence
- Main claims and argument chain.
- Empirical, conceptual, or normative premises.
- Counterarguments considered by the authors.
- Falsifiable predictions or concrete implications.
- Scope and value judgments.

## Required reasoning checks
- Which claims are empirical and which are normative?
- What would change the author's mind?
- Is the strongest counterargument addressed?

## Common weakest assumptions
- The central framing captures the important variables.
- Normative premises are shared by the target audience.
- The argument would survive strong counterexamples.

## Minimal reproduction
Build an argument map and identify one falsifiable prediction or decision consequence.

Support and falsification criteria may be conceptual consistency checks, concrete decision consequences, or cases that would make the framing recommend the wrong action.

## Strong counterexample
A concrete case where the framing predicts the wrong action or ignores a decisive factor.

## Suitable learning artifacts
Argument map, counterexample checker, or interactive claim classifier.

## Do not force
Do not invent experiments when the paper only makes conceptual claims.
